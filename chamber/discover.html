5 Facts about São Luís

  1. The Only Brazilian City Founded by the French

    2. São Luís was founded in 1612 by French explorers under the command of Daniel de La Touche, making it the only Brazilian city with French origins.

  2. Known as the "City of Tiles"

    3. The city is famous for its azulejos (Portuguese tiles) that adorn many of its colonial buildings, earning it the nickname "City of Tiles."

  3. Linguistic Influence

    4. The city has a unique accent and vocabulary influenced by French, Portuguese, and African cultures, making its Portuguese distinct from other regions.

  4. São Luís Is Located on an Island"

    5. The city is on São Luís Island, also known as Upaon-Açu, a Tupi-Guarani name meaning "Big Island."

  5. High Tide Variance

    6. São Luís experiences one of the highest tidal variations in Brazil, which can reach up to 7 meters during full and new moons.

Historic Center of São Luís is

The Historic Center of São Luís is a UNESCO World Heritage Site, renowned for its charming colonial architecture and vibrant azulejos. Its cobblestone streets tell the story of the city's rich cultural and historical heritage.
